About this report

What this shows. Each dot is one completed item: x = completion date, y = cycle time (days from creation to completion). Horizontal percentile lines mark probability thresholds — a new item entering the system has an 85% chance of finishing in P85 days or less, by definition of the percentile.

How to read the percentile lines.

  • The lines are EMPIRICAL — drawn at the cycle times that separate the finished items into ranked groups. P50 = half the items finished at or below this number of days; P85 = 85% of items finished at or below this number; etc.
  • By definition, a new item entering the system has the matching probability of finishing in that time or less. P85 (13.3d for this window) is the conventional external-commitment line.
  • The deep tail (above P95) is where retrospectives find the most leverage — what happened with each of those items, and can we prevent it next time?

Key insight. Median cycle time is 1.7 days; deep-tail P95 is 36.5 days. A new item entering the system has, by definition, an 85% chance of finishing in 13.3 days or less and a 50% chance of finishing in 1.7 days or less.

Next actions

  1. Use P85 (13.3d) as the external commitment for new work — promising P50 builds a 50% miss rate into the plan.
  2. Re-check at the end of each iteration to spot trends; a rising P85 over consecutive windows means the system is slowing down.

Caveats

  • Percentiles are EMPIRICAL — read from the actual finished items in this window. No simulation, no estimation.
  • Outliers (very long cycle times) shift P85 / P95 up. If the window is short or includes a single stuck-then-finished PR, the deep-tail percentiles can be noisy.

Vocabulary

Cycle Time
Days from when a work item entered the system (created) to when it left (merged / resolved). One dot on the chart per completed item; the dot's y-position is its cycle time.
Percentile Line
A horizontal line on the scatterplot such that the given percent of dots fall on or below it. P50 = median cycle time; 85% of completed items finished in P85 days or less. A new item entering the system has, by definition, the matching probability of finishing in that time.
Completion date
When the item was merged (GitHub) or resolved (Jira). The dot's x-position on the chart.
